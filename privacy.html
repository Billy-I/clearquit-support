Privacy Policy

Effective 10 July 2026

Overview

ClearQuit is designed to keep personal quit-tracking information private. The app does not sell personal information, show advertising or track you across apps and websites.

Information stored on your device

Your quit plan, usage estimates, check-ins, craving sessions, relapse records, goals, milestones, theme choice and reminder preferences are stored locally on your device using Apple frameworks. ClearQuit does not send this information to the developer.

Notifications

If you enable reminders, ClearQuit asks iOS for notification permission and schedules notifications locally on your device. You can disable them in ClearQuit or iOS Settings.

Subscriptions and RevenueCat

ClearQuit uses RevenueCat to offer and manage ClearQuit Pro. RevenueCat receives purchase history and an anonymous app user identifier so it can validate purchases, provide subscription functionality and report aggregate subscription analytics. ClearQuit does not attach your name or email address to that identifier.

Apple processes App Store payments. ClearQuit and RevenueCat do not receive your full payment-card details.

Website

This support website does not use analytics, advertising pixels, accounts or cookies set by ClearQuit. GitHub Pages may process basic technical request information to operate and secure the hosting service.

Retention and deletion

Local tracking information remains on your device until you remove it or delete the app. Subscription records are retained by Apple and RevenueCat as needed to operate purchases, prevent fraud and meet legal obligations. You can manage subscriptions from your Apple Account subscription settings.

Children

ClearQuit is not directed to children under 13. It is a general self-tracking tool and does not knowingly collect identifying information from children.

Changes

Material changes will be reflected on this page with a revised effective date.
